# Daedalus Kernel — Governance Lineage

The Daedalus Kernel is the **single governance core** of the system.  
It defines what is allowed, what is forbidden, and what must always be visible.

The Kernel era is a **lineage**, not just a module:

1. KernelShell  
2. KernelInvariants  
3. Kernel  
4. KernelHalo  
5. KernelCrown  
6. KernelThrone  
7. KernelRite  
8. KernelSeal  

---

## KernelShell

Boundary and intake gate.  
Routes all inputs through ConnectivityGate and EpistemicFilter.  
Never makes decisions; enforces rules.

---

## KernelInvariants

Constitutional rules governing:

- Sovereignty  
- Epistemic integrity  
- Continuity  
- Node trust  
- Governance safety  

Cannot be changed after the Seal.

---

## Kernel

Governance core.  
Holds authoritative state.  
Feeds Halo and Crown.  
Never expresses directly.

---

## KernelHalo

Introspection surface.  
Exposes raw state:

- Connectivity  
- Epistemic  
- Operator  
- Being  
- Nodes  
- Attention  
- Continuity  
- Pre‑Seal Validation (pre‑Seal only)

---

## KernelCrown

Expressive mapping of Kernel state:

- Glows  
- Pulses  
- Shading  
- Continuity modulation  

Never feeds back into governance.

---

## KernelThrone

Operator cockpit:

- Operator context  
- Node presence  
- Attention & tasks  
- Continuity  
- Epistemic signals  
- Pre‑Seal status  

---

## KernelRite

Activation ritual.  
Ensures correct initialization order.

---

## KernelSeal

Irreversible closure of the governance lineage.  
Freezes all Kernel semantics.

See `docs/seal/SEAL.md`.

---

- Single Kernel
