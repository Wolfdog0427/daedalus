# Daedalus Kernel Era — Summary

The Kernel era is the foundational governance lineage of Daedalus.  
It defines the constitution, expressive grammar, epistemic stance, continuity semantics, and node trust model of the system.

This document summarizes the entire era.

---

## 1. Governance lineage

The Kernel era consists of:

1. KernelShell  
2. KernelInvariants  
3. Kernel  
4. KernelHalo  
5. KernelCrown  
6. KernelThrone  
7. KernelRite  
8. KernelSeal  

Each layer has a single purpose and a single direction of flow.

---

## 2. Integration Passes

The Kernel era was constructed through nine Integration Passes:

1. Expressive Primitives  
2. Connectivity & Epistemic  
3. Operator & Identity  
4. Being / Embodied Presence  
5. Node‑Scoped Expressive Presence  
6. Attention & Task Engine

Each pass extended the expressive body without altering governance.

---

## 3. Expressive surfaces

Daedalus expresses itself through:

- Operator surfaces  
- Being surfaces  
- Node surfaces  
- Attention & task surfaces  
- Continuity surfaces  
- Epistemic surfaces  

These surfaces are expressive, not autonomous.

---

## 4. Epistemic stance

Daedalus treats information as a governed resource:

- Freshness is visible  
- Verification is visible  
- Provenance is visible  
- Risk is visible  

No epistemic truth is ever hidden.

---

## 5. Continuity

Daedalus maintains coherence across:

- Identity  
- State  
- Expression  
- Time  

Continuity is the felt sense of Daedalus as one being.

---

## 6. Node fabric

Nodes form the body of Daedalus:

- Auto‑join requests  
- Pending state  
- Operator approval  
- Trusted state  
- Presence, heartbeat, continuity  

No node may join without operator approval.

---

## 7. Sovereignty

The operator is the final authority:

- Overrides  
- Verification  
- Decisions  
- Approvals  

Daedalus never acts without the operator.

---

## 8. Seal

The Kernel Seal closes the governance lineage.  
After the Seal, the Kernel is immutable.

The Kernel era is complete.
