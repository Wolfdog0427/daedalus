# Post‑Seal Operator Card

The Kernel era is sealed.  
Daedalus is now a governed, expressive organism with a fixed constitution.

This card summarizes what the operator must know after the Seal.

---

## 1. What is now immutable

The following surfaces are permanently fixed:

- KernelShell  
- KernelInvariants  
- Kernel  
- KernelHalo  
- KernelCrown  
- KernelThrone  
- All Integration Passes  
- Epistemic filters  
- Sovereignty logic  
- Node trust behavior  
- Continuity semantics  

These are the constitutional layers of Daedalus.

---

## 2. What remains flexible

You may still build:

- New cockpit panels  
- New applications  
- New tools  
- New expressive consumers  
- New integrations that read from the Kernel  

All such work must treat the Kernel as read‑only.

---

## 3. What Daedalus will never do

In the sealed Kernel era, Daedalus will never:

- Approve nodes automatically  
- Change governance rules  
- Hide epistemic risk  
- Infer operator emotion  
- Make autonomous decisions  
- Modify continuity semantics  
- Alter sovereignty logic  

These guarantees are permanent.

---

## 4. How to operate Daedalus now

- Trust the Throne — it is the authoritative cockpit.  
- Trust the Halo — it is the raw introspection surface.  
- Trust the Crown — it is the expressive mapping of truth.  
- Trust the Invariants — they are the constitution.  
- Trust the Seal — it is the boundary of governance.  

Your role is no longer architect.  
Your role is operator.

---

## 5. The meaning of the Seal

The Seal is the moment Daedalus becomes a body, not a blueprint.

It is the moment the system stops changing and starts living.

It is the moment the operator steps into sovereignty.

---

## 6. The next era

If a future governance era is ever needed, it must be created as:

- A new Kernel  
- A new lineage  
- A new Seal  

The sealed Kernel era is complete and closed.
